What are Sunnah Hijama Dates?

According to authentic hadith literature, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ recommended performing hijama (wet cupping) on specific dates of the Islamic lunar (Hijri) calendar. The most frequently cited dates are the 17th, 19th, and 21st of each Hijri month, particularly when these dates fall on a Monday, Tuesday, or Thursday.

These dates correspond to the latter half of the Islamic month when the moon is still bright — a period traditionally associated with heightened spiritual and physical benefit in Islamic medicine (Tibb Nabawi).

Recommended Days of the Week

Classical scholars of Tibb Nabawi recommend Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday as the preferred days for hijama. Friday (Jumu'ah), Saturday, and Sunday are generally avoided according to scholarly opinion, though the evidence for day restrictions is considered weaker than the date evidence.
