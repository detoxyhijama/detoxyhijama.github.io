Hijama Cups: Types, Materials and Applications

Silicone Cups (Medical-Grade): The most versatile and widely used cup type in modern hijama practice. Medical-grade silicone cups — particularly platinum-cured silicone which is free of chemical leaching risks — are suitable for dry cupping, wet hijama, and moving cupping techniques. They require no pump or special equipment, are applied by squeezing and releasing, and are autoclave-sterilisable to 134°C. Available in Size 1 through Size 6 (and specialist Curve Cup sizes for joints and contoured surfaces), silicone cups should be BPA-free, latex-free, and translucent to allow visual monitoring of skin response during treatment.

Glass Fire Cupping Sets: Borosilicate glass cups are the traditional instrument of authentic Sunnah hijama and remain the preferred choice of many experienced practitioners. The fire technique — briefly introducing flame into the cup before placing it on the skin — creates superior suction quality compared to mechanical methods and provides the most authentic traditional hijama experience. Borosilicate glass is essential (not soda-lime glass which shatters under thermal stress) and should be at least 3mm wall thickness for safety. Glass cups allow visual assessment of skin response, have a superior clinical feel, and are favoured for their association with traditional prophetic medicine.

Vacuum Pump Kits (Magnetic Lock System): Vacuum pump kits use valve-top cups connected to a hand pump for precise, measured suction control. The magnetic lock vacuum pump mechanism provides click-by-click pressure increments, allowing exact replication of treatment parameters between sessions. These kits are ideal for clinical settings where treatment standardisation and outcome tracking are priorities. A 24-cup magnetic vacuum kit provides four graduated sizes covering the full anatomical range of hijama practice.

Electric Cupping Devices: Battery-powered electric cupping devices with adjustable suction levels (typically 9 levels) and optional heat function represent the most modern form of cupping technology. They are particularly valuable for elderly patients, first-time patients who may find the sensation of manual cups intimidating, and long-duration sessions where maintaining consistent suction pressure is important. The LCD display, auto-pressure control, and USB-C recharging of advanced models provide a professional, clinical appearance.

Bamboo Cups: Hand-crafted from mature Moso bamboo, bamboo cups represent the traditional alternative medicine tradition and are particularly favoured by practitioners combining hijama with other traditional healing modalities. Bamboo cups are used with steam heating technique and provide a unique therapeutic experience valued in holistic and traditional practice contexts.

Silicone Facial Cups: Specifically designed for facial cupping with smaller diameters (1.5–6cm) and softer silicone than body cups. Available in 4-piece and 7-piece sets covering the full range of facial applications from periorbital work to décolletage treatment.

Silicone Moving Cups: Flexible cups designed for the gliding massage cupping technique. Used with massage oil for myofascial release across broad tissue areas.

Suction Gun: The suction gun (also called hijama vacuum gun) is an ergonomic pistol-grip tool that connects to valve-top cups to apply and release vacuum with precise single-handed control. Essential equipment when using valve-top cups with a vacuum pump kit.

Incision Instruments for Wet Hijama

Auto Lancet Pen: The modern standard for wet hijama incisions. Reusable pen body with disposable, sterile single-use lancet heads. Depth-adjustable (typically 5 settings from 0.8mm to 1.8mm) for precise control across different patient demographics and anatomical areas. Spring-loaded mechanism produces consistent, controlled incision depth with minimal practitioner variation — critical for both safety and standardised outcomes. The auto lancet pen is the preferred tool for practitioners who see multiple patients daily.

Surgical Blades: Individually gamma-irradiated sterile surgical blades (Size 22 for standard adult hijama, Size 15 for finer work) in peel-open sterile packaging. Carbon steel construction maintains the sharpest cutting edge of any blade material. Strictly single-use — never reuse any blade. Safe disposal in a rigid sharps container is mandatory after use.

Protective Equipment and Consumables

Latex Examination Gloves: Medical-grade latex gloves provide the critical barrier protection required when working with blood and body fluids during wet hijama. Textured fingertips are essential for tactile precision during lancet use and cup placement. Available in medium and large sizes. 100 gloves per box (50 pairs).

Surgical Cotton: Medical-grade 400g cotton rolls for skin preparation (soaked in antiseptic), post-incision blood absorption, and post-procedure wound care. The lint-free quality of medical-grade cotton is essential for wound contact — craft or household cotton leaves fibres in wounds.

Antiseptic Solutions: Chlorhexidine gluconate 2% or povidone-iodine 10% for pre-procedure skin preparation. 70% isopropyl alcohol for equipment surface disinfection. Proper skin antisepsis before hijama incisions is a fundamental infection-control requirement.

Silicone cups and autoclave-safe plastic cups can be sterilised in an autoclave at 121–134°C.
