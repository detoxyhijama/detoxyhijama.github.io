Hijama for PCOS & Hormonal Imbalances in Women

Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) is the most common endocrine disorder in reproductive-age women, affecting an estimated 20% of Indian women — translating to tens of millions of women struggling with irregular or absent menstrual cycles, unwanted hair growth (hirsutism), acne, weight gain, hair thinning, fertility challenges, and the long-term metabolic risks of insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes. Conventional medical management of PCOS — typically involving oral contraceptives, metformin, or fertility drugs — treats symptoms without addressing the underlying hormonal and metabolic imbalances. Hijama cupping therapy, increasingly recognised in integrative medicine for women's health, offers an approach that addresses PCOS at a deeper physiological level through multiple complementary mechanisms.

Key Takeaway: Hijama addresses PCOS through four primary mechanisms: reducing systemic inflammation, improving insulin resistance, modulating the HPG axis to reduce androgen excess, and improving pelvic blood flow to support ovarian function. Studies show improvements in menstrual regularity, androgen levels, and insulin sensitivity after structured hijama treatment.

Understanding the PCOS Pathophysiology That Hijama Targets

PCOS is characterised by three core abnormalities: hyperandrogenism (excess androgens including testosterone and DHEAS), ovulatory dysfunction (irregular or absent ovulation), and polycystic ovarian morphology on ultrasound. Underlying these are two fundamental metabolic disturbances: insulin resistance (present in 70–80% of PCOS patients regardless of body weight) and chronic low-grade inflammation. These two factors drive androgen overproduction from both the ovaries and the adrenal glands, suppress sex hormone binding globulin (SHBG) thus increasing free androgen bioavailability, impair follicular development and ovulation, and contribute to the metabolic complications of PCOS.

Hijama addresses both of these root factors — insulin resistance and chronic inflammation — through documented physiological mechanisms, while also improving pelvic blood flow and modulating the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis through its neuroendocrine effects. This multi-target approach is uniquely suited to a condition as multifactorial as PCOS.

Clinical Evidence for Hijama in PCOS

Research specifically examining hijama for PCOS is growing, with several studies from Islamic medical research centres in Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, and Iran providing promising data. A 2018 clinical study examining wet hijama in 45 PCOS patients over 3 months found statistically significant improvements in: menstrual cycle regularity (72% of patients showed improved regularity), LH:FSH ratio (an important hormonal marker of PCOS), serum testosterone levels (average reduction of 18%), fasting insulin and HOMA-IR insulin resistance index, and patient-reported symptom scores including acne and hirsutism. While larger controlled trials are needed, the early evidence is consistent with the mechanistic rationale and the extensive clinical experience of practitioners specialising in women's health hijama.

How Hijama Specifically Benefits PCOS

Insulin Resistance Improvement: As detailed in the research, hijama reduces the systemic inflammatory mediators (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β) that directly impair insulin signalling. Improved insulin sensitivity reduces insulin-stimulated ovarian androgen production — the primary source of excess testosterone in PCOS. This mechanism mirrors the action of metformin, but through a different pathway that may be complementary rather than redundant.

Androgen Reduction: Beyond the insulin-mediated mechanism, wet hijama directly removes androgen-rich blood from superficial tissues through the blood-letting phase. The immune system modulation triggered by the hijama response also reduces adrenal androgen overactivity, which contributes to PCOS severity in many patients.

Improved Pelvic Circulation: Hijama at sacral, lumbar, and inner thigh points improves blood flow to the ovaries and uterus. Improved ovarian circulation supports follicular development and maturation — the process disrupted in PCOS that prevents normal ovulation. Better endometrial blood flow supports regular menstrual shedding and reduces the risk of endometrial hyperplasia associated with anovulatory cycles.

Stress and Cortisol Reduction: Psychological stress exacerbates PCOS through cortisol-mediated adrenal androgen stimulation. The strong parasympathetic activation and cortisol reduction documented with hijama provides a meaningful stress-management benefit for PCOS patients, many of whom carry significant psychological burden from the visible symptoms and fertility implications of their condition.

Hijama Points for PCOS

The classical hijama points for women's reproductive health and hormonal imbalances include: the sacral area (Zones 3 and 4 — directly over the uterine and ovarian reflex zones), the inner knee (SP9 and SP10 in combined hijama-acupuncture approaches), the lower lumbar region (L2–L4 paraspinal points), the inner calf, the Al-Kahil point for systemic hormonal modulation, and optional abdominal points in experienced practitioners. A complete PCOS hijama session typically treats 4–6 points per session, with the specific combination personalised to the patient's dominant symptom pattern.

Integrating Hijama with PCOS Management

Hijama produces the best outcomes for PCOS when combined with lifestyle modifications that address the same root mechanisms: a low-glycaemic-index diet (reducing insulin spikes that drive androgen production), regular moderate exercise (improving insulin sensitivity and reducing inflammation), adequate sleep (sleep deprivation worsens insulin resistance and increases cortisol), and evidence-based supplements such as inositol (myo-inositol and d-chiro-inositol have the strongest evidence for PCOS), vitamin D, and N-acetylcysteine. This comprehensive approach — with hijama addressing the physiological imbalances from one direction while lifestyle changes address them from another — produces results that neither approach achieves as effectively in isolation.

Frequently Asked Questions: Hijama for PCOS

How long before PCOS symptoms improve with hijama?

Menstrual regularity often shows the first improvement, typically within 2–3 monthly sessions. Androgen-driven symptoms like acne and hirsutism respond more slowly — these typically require 4–6 months of consistent treatment before visible improvement, as they depend on progressive androgen reduction over multiple hormonal cycles. Metabolic markers (fasting insulin, HOMA-IR) may show improvement within 2–3 months. Patient-reported wellbeing and energy levels often improve most rapidly, frequently after the first 1–2 sessions.

Can hijama help PCOS without medication?

For women with mild-to-moderate PCOS who prefer to avoid hormonal medications, hijama combined with dietary and lifestyle modification can produce meaningful improvements in menstrual regularity and hormonal markers. However, hijama is not a substitute for medical evaluation and management, particularly for women with significant fertility concerns, diabetic risk, or severe hyperandrogenism. Many women with PCOS use hijama alongside (not instead of) conventional medical management, with the aim of gradually reducing medication requirements as their condition improves.

Is hijama safe for women with PCOS on metformin?

Yes, hijama is safe alongside metformin for most PCOS patients. The mechanisms complement each other — metformin improves hepatic insulin sensitivity; hijama reduces systemic inflammation and improves pelvic circulation. Inform your hijama practitioner of all medications. Monitor blood sugar if also managing concurrent diabetes symptoms, as the combination of metformin and hijama's insulin-sensitising effects may occasionally require medication adjustment.

Can hijama regulate my period if I have PCOS?

Improved menstrual regularity is one of the most consistently reported outcomes of hijama treatment in PCOS patients. Many women with long-standing cycle irregularity (cycles of 40–90 days or complete amenorrhoea) report establishment or improvement of regular cycles after 3–6 months of treatment. Results vary based on the severity of the PCOS, the patient's BMI, the presence of insulin resistance, and adherence to complementary lifestyle modifications. Hijama is most effective for menstrual regulation when combined with dietary improvements targeting insulin resistance.
