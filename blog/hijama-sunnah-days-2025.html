Hijama Sunnah Days 2025: Complete Lunar Calendar Guide

One of the most frequently asked questions among Muslim patients seeking hijama is: "When are the Sunnah days for hijama?" The Islamic prophetic tradition provides specific guidance on the optimal days of the lunar calendar for hijama treatment, with the 17th, 19th, and 21st of every month in the Islamic (Hijri) calendar identified as the most beneficial days. This comprehensive guide provides the Sunnah hijama days for every month of 2025, along with the Islamic scholarly basis for these recommendations and practical guidance for scheduling your sessions.

Prophetic Hadith: "Whoever performs hijama on the 17th, 19th, and 21st day of the month will be cured from every disease." — Sunan Abu Dawud 3861. The Prophet ﷺ also preferred Monday and Thursday for hijama.

The Basis for Sunnah Hijama Days

The recommendation of the 17th, 19th, and 21st of the Islamic lunar month comes from several authenticated hadith in the major hadith collections. These days in the lunar calendar correspond to the waxing gibbous to full moon phase, when the gravitational tidal effects on body fluids are at their peak — causing blood to rise toward the body's surface and accumulate in the superficial capillary beds. This physiological state, in which blood is more accessible in the subcutaneous tissue, is believed to make hijama more effective in drawing out dam fasid (stagnant blood) and metabolic waste from these superficial vessels. Contemporary chrobiologists and integrative medicine researchers are beginning to investigate the lunar cycle's influence on human physiology, providing a scientific framework for what the Prophet ﷺ observed and taught 1,400 years ago.

The preference for Monday and Thursday aligns with the days the Prophet ﷺ is reported to have fasted and performed other acts of worship, suggesting a connection between these days and elevated spiritual and physical wellbeing in the Islamic tradition. The ideal Sunnah hijama appointment is on the 17th, 19th, or 21st of an Islamic month that falls on a Monday or Thursday.

Frequently Asked Questions: Hijama Sunnah Days

What if I can't get hijama exactly on the Sunnah days?

The Sunnah day recommendation is preferred (mustahabb) but not obligatory. Can I get hijama on days other than Monday and Thursday?

Yes. The preference for Monday and Thursday is a recommendation of excellence, not a restriction. Hijama is permissible and beneficial on any day of the week. Wednesday is mentioned in some narrations as a day to approach with more caution, but scholars differ on the interpretation of these reports. Most contemporary hijama practitioners schedule sessions based on patient and clinic availability.

Is it better to get hijama before or after Ramadan?

Both have strong traditional recommendations. Pre-Ramadan hijama (in Sha'ban) prepares the body for the rigour of fasting by clearing metabolic waste, improving circulation, and boosting energy. Post-Ramadan hijama (in Shawwal) provides recovery and renewal after the month of intensive worship and dietary changes. Many dedicated practitioners recommend hijama in both Sha'ban and Shawwal as an annual health maintenance practice.

Does it matter if the Sunnah day falls during my menstrual cycle?

Islamic scholarly opinion generally holds that a woman in a state of menstruation (haydh) can receive hijama — menstruation does not invalidate or prohibit hijama. The treatment points and protocol may be modified by an experienced female practitioner during this time. Consult your preferred Islamic scholar for guidance specific to your madhab (jurisprudential school).
