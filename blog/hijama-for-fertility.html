Hijama for Fertility & Reproductive Health

Fertility challenges affect an estimated 15–20% of couples in India, and the emotional, financial, and psychological burden of infertility is profound. As conventional medical fertility treatments (IUI, IVF) become increasingly accessible, many couples are simultaneously exploring complementary approaches to optimise their reproductive health and improve the outcomes of medical fertility interventions. Hijama cupping therapy is one of the most well-regarded complementary approaches in Islamic medicine for supporting reproductive health in both men and women — backed by both the prophetic tradition and a growing body of modern clinical research.

Key Takeaway: Hijama supports fertility by improving pelvic blood circulation, reducing chronic inflammation affecting reproductive organs, modulating hormonal balance through the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis, and addressing specific conditions like PCOS, endometriosis, and male factor infertility. It is most effective as part of a comprehensive fertility support programme.

How Hijama Supports Female Fertility

Improved Pelvic Circulation: Adequate blood flow to the uterus, ovaries, and fallopian tubes is fundamental to reproductive health. Conditions including endometriosis, uterine fibroids, and chronic pelvic inflammatory disease are associated with impaired pelvic circulation and increased local inflammation. Hijama applied to specific pelvic and sacral points significantly improves blood flow to the pelvic organs, creating a more favourable environment for follicular development, ovulation, and endometrial receptivity (the ability of the uterine lining to support implantation of a fertilised egg).

Hormonal Balance: The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis — the hormonal cascade controlling the menstrual cycle, ovulation, and fertility — is exquisitely sensitive to inflammatory disruption, stress hormone levels, and autonomic nervous system tone. Hijama's documented effects on reducing cortisol, modulating the HPG axis, and improving autonomic balance create conditions that support more regular ovulation and cycle regularity in women with hormonal imbalances.

PCOS Management: Polycystic ovarian syndrome is one of the most common causes of female infertility in India, affecting an estimated 20% of reproductive-age women. Hijama has shown specific benefits for PCOS including: reduction in androgen levels (testosterone and DHEAS that suppress ovulation), improvement in insulin resistance (a central feature of PCOS), enhancement of LH:FSH ratio normalisation, and improvement in menstrual regularity. Many women with PCOS in India have reported significant improvements in cycle regularity and ovulation frequency following a course of hijama treatments.

Endometriosis Support: Endometriosis — the presence of uterine lining tissue outside the uterus — is a significant cause of infertility associated with chronic pelvic inflammation, adhesions, and impaired fallopian tube function. While hijama cannot physically remove endometriotic implants, its anti-inflammatory effects, pelvic circulation improvement, and immune modulation can reduce the inflammatory burden of the condition and improve the hormonal environment for fertility.

How Hijama Supports Male Fertility

Male factor infertility — contributing to 40–50% of all infertility cases — includes reduced sperm count (oligospermia), poor sperm motility (asthenospermia), abnormal sperm morphology (teratospermia), and erectile dysfunction. Hijama addresses several underlying factors in male infertility: improving testicular circulation (reduced blood flow is associated with reduced sperm production and quality), reducing oxidative stress in seminal fluid (elevated reactive oxygen species damage sperm DNA and impair motility), and modulating hormonal balance including testosterone and FSH levels. Clinical studies examining wet hijama for male infertility have reported improvements in sperm count, motility, and morphology following a structured treatment course.

Hijama Points for Fertility

Traditional Islamic medicine identifies specific therapeutic points for reproductive health. For female fertility, the primary points include: the sacral area (directly over the uterine and ovarian reflex zones), the inner knee and inner calf (classical reproductive health points in Islamic medicine), specific lower abdominal points, and systemic points including Al-Kahil for hormonal modulation. For male fertility, testicular reflex points in the sacral and lumbar region, inner thigh points, and systemic points are combined. Point selection is always individualised based on the practitioner's assessment of the patient's specific condition.

Hijama as IVF Support

A growing number of fertility specialists and hijama practitioners in India are collaborating to integrate hijama as part of IVF (in vitro fertilisation) preparation protocols. Pre-IVF hijama treatment aims to: improve uterine blood flow and endometrial receptivity, reduce systemic inflammation that can impair implantation, optimise hormonal environment for follicular stimulation, and reduce the stress and anxiety associated with fertility treatment. Several fertility clinics have reported improved IVF success rates in patients who received preparatory hijama sessions compared to those who did not, though large controlled studies are still needed to confirm these clinical observations.

Frequently Asked Questions: Hijama and Fertility

How long before trying to conceive should I start hijama?

For optimal fertility support, beginning hijama 3–6 months before actively trying to conceive is recommended. This timeframe allows enough treatment sessions to establish improved pelvic circulation, hormonal balance, and immune modulation. For couples preparing for IVF, beginning hijama 3 months before the planned cycle is a commonly recommended timeline among integrative fertility practitioners.

Can hijama be done during pregnancy?

Wet hijama on the abdomen and lower back is contraindicated during pregnancy. Dry cupping on the upper back and shoulders may be appropriate in some circumstances but should only be performed by a practitioner experienced in treating pregnant patients and with the knowledge of the obstetric care team. The first trimester in particular requires extreme caution. Many practitioners completely avoid treating pregnant patients to eliminate any risk.

Can hijama help if I have PCOS and am trying to conceive?

Yes, hijama is one of the most promising complementary approaches for PCOS-related subfertility in Islamic medicine. It addresses the key drivers of PCOS (insulin resistance, androgen excess, chronic inflammation, poor pelvic circulation) through multiple mechanisms. Women with PCOS seeking to conceive should combine hijama with dietary improvements (particularly reducing sugar and refined carbohydrates), appropriate prescribed medication, and regular exercise for maximum fertility support.

Is hijama recommended for male fertility problems?

Yes. Hijama is recommended in the Islamic medical tradition for male sexual and reproductive health, and modern clinical research supports its benefits for sperm quality and count. Men with diagnosed male factor infertility should consider a 3-month course of monthly wet hijama alongside their conventional fertility investigation and treatment, as it addresses several underlying factors (circulation, oxidative stress, hormonal balance) that are not typically targeted by conventional male fertility treatments.
